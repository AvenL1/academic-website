<!-- Syllabus: EIE2211 -->
<div id="syllabus-eie2211" style="background: #fcfdfd; border: 1px solid #e2ece9; border-left: 5px solid #005A32; padding: 22px 26px; border-radius: 4px; margin-bottom: 30px; scroll-margin-top: 80px;">
  <h4 style="font-weight: bold; color: #005A32; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 15px; font-size: 1.2rem;">EIE2211: Logic Design</h4>
  <table style="width: 100%; border-collapse: collapse; margin-bottom: 15px; font-size: 0.95rem;">
    <tr style="border-bottom: 1px solid #f0f4f2;"><td style="padding: 6px 0; font-weight: bold; width: 120px; color: #444;">Credit Value:</td><td style="padding: 6px 0; color: #222;">3</td></tr>
    <tr style="border-bottom: 1px solid #f0f4f2;"><td style="padding: 6px 0; font-weight: bold; color: #444;">Level:</td><td style="padding: 6px 0; color: #222;">2</td></tr>
  </table>
  <div style="font-size: 0.95rem; color: #333;">
    <p style="margin-bottom: 8px;"><strong>Objectives:</strong></p>
    <p style="color: #444; margin-top: 0; text-align: justify;">To provide students with a broad view in both hardware and software aspects of digital systems in general and microprocessor systems in particular, and enable them to gain understanding and skills that will be used in later computer related courses.</p>
    <p style="margin-bottom: 4px;"><strong>Indicative Syllabus:</strong></p>
    <ul style="margin-top: 0; padding-left: 20px; color: #444;">
      <li><strong>Logic Circuit and ICs:</strong> Decoders, encoders, multiplexers, demultiplexers, binary adders, adder-subtractors, binary multipliers, sequential circuit analysis and design, registers, counters, and HDL representation.</li>
      <li><strong>Memory & Programmable Logic Devices:</strong> RAM operations, timing waveforms, RAM integrated circuits, three-state buffers, DRAM ICs, ROM, PLA, PAL, and VLSI programmable logic devices (Xilinx FPGA).</li>
      <li><strong>Microprocessor:</strong> Register transfer operations, microoperations, bus-based transfer, ALU, shifter, datapath representation, control word, control unit (hardwired control), and Basic Assembly Language Programming.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

<!-- Syllabus: EIE577 -->
<div id="syllabus-eie577" style="background: #fcfdfd; border: 1px solid #e2ece9; border-left: 5px solid #005A32; padding: 22px 26px; border-radius: 4px; margin-bottom: 30px; scroll-margin-top: 80px;">
  <h4 style="font-weight: bold; color: #005A32; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 15px; font-size: 1.2rem;">EIE577: Optoelectronic Devices</h4>
  <table style="width: 100%; border-collapse: collapse; margin-bottom: 15px; font-size: 0.95rem;">
    <tr style="border-bottom: 1px solid #f0f4f2;"><td style="padding: 6px 0; font-weight: bold; width: 120px; color: #444;">Credit Value:</td><td style="padding: 6px 0; color: #222;">3</td></tr>
    <tr style="border-bottom: 1px solid #f0f4f2;"><td style="padding: 6px 0; font-weight: bold; color: #444;">Level:</td><td style="padding: 6px 0; color: #222;">5</td></tr>
  </table>
  <div style="font-size: 0.95rem; color: #333;">
    <p style="margin-bottom: 8px;"><strong>Objectives:</strong></p>
    <p style="color: #444; margin-top: 0; text-align: justify;">The aim of this course is to introduce to the students to the fundamentals of semiconductor optoelectronic devices. These include pn junctions, light emitting diodes (LEDs) and solar cells.</p>
    <p style="margin-bottom: 4px;"><strong>Indicative Syllabus:</strong></p>
    <ul style="margin-top: 0; padding-left: 20px; color: #444;">
      <li><strong>Elements of Wave Mechanics:</strong> The Bohr atom, wave-particle duality, general formulation, particle in a 1-D box.</li>
      <li><strong>Basic Energy Band Theory:</strong> The Bloch theorem, Kronig-Penny model, energy bands and Brillouin zones, particle motion and effective mass, E-k diagrams, band gap energy.</li>
      <li><strong>Semiconductor Fundamentals:</strong> Basics of electrical and optical properties of semiconductor materials, P-N junctions.</li>
      <li><strong>Semiconductor LEDs:</strong> Operation principles of LEDs, human vision, photometry and colorimetry, white solid-state lamps (phosphor conversion versus multichip LEDs), display fundamentals.</li>
      <li><strong>Solar Cells and Photodetectors:</strong> Operation principles of solar cells, silicon-based solar cells, compound semiconductor based solar cells.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>
